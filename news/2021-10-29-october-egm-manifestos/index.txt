c:T100f,<h2>Event Details</h2>
<p>On Friday (today) at 18:30 we have this year's first monthly Student Tech Meetup as well as our Emergency General Meeting to elect some new committee members! Here are the session details, and the election manifestos.</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Venue:</strong> Online (on <a href="https://www.twitch.tv/compsocedinburgh">Twitch</a> and <a href="https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCOEMTnIKR29AnSVVdLQZg3Q">YouTube</a>). No tickets required!</li>
<li><strong>Time:</strong> 18:30 - est. 20:30 BST</li>
</ul>
<h3>Programme</h3>
<p><strong>Dylan Thinnes</strong> - Why We Learn Languages</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Our programming languages are Turing-complete, meaning they all can solve the same problems - so why do we bother to learn more than one?<br>
In this talk, we'll go over how programming languages shape (for better or worse) the way we think about problems.<br>
We'll go over languages you have heard of, some that are more obscure, and even some "joke" languages, and see what they all bring to the table.</p>
</blockquote>
<p><strong>CompSoc Committee</strong> - Election</p>
<blockquote>
<p>We're holding an election to elect a new Vice President, 1st Year Representative, and 3rd Year Representative.<br>
Candidates will give 1 minute speeches (2 minutes for VP), and the audience may be ask questions.<br>
See below for details.</p>
</blockquote>
<h2>Election Details</h2>
